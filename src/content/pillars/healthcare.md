For most people, healthcare is the deciding factor in whether retiring abroad works long-term. Pattaya is well served by private hospitals with international standards, and routine care is often faster and far cheaper than back home.

But we won't sugar-coat the part that matters most: **health insurance gets more expensive — and harder to get — as you age**, and pre-existing conditions are frequently excluded. A plan that's affordable in your fifties can become eye-watering in your seventies, and some insurers stop offering new cover past a certain age.

This is the most carefully-sourced section on the site. We'll cover the main hospitals, realistic costs, how insurance works by age band, and the uncomfortable question everyone should think about before they move: *what's my plan if I can't get, or can't afford, cover later?*
