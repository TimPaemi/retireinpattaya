The climate is one of Pattaya's biggest draws — and a real adjustment. Knowing the rhythm of the seasons helps you plan both your visit and your move.

## The three rough seasons

- **Cooler & drier (around Nov–Feb):** the most comfortable stretch, and peak season. Pleasant days, lower humidity.
- **Hot season (around Mar–May):** genuinely hot and humid — the real test of whether the tropics suit you.
- **Rainy season (around Jun–Oct):** heavy but often **short** downpours rather than all-day rain, with warm temperatures throughout.

## Honest climate expectations

This is a **tropical climate**: warm to hot every month, with real humidity. Some people bloom in it; others find the relentless heat wearing. The only way to know which you are is to experience more than the pleasant cool season — ideally visit in the heat too.

## Best time to arrive

For the **settling-in admin** — house-hunting, banking, visa errands — the **cooler season** is far more pleasant to be running around in. But there's a case for visiting in the hot and wet months *first*, so you commit with a true picture of the climate, not just the postcard version.

## A note on health and heat

If you have heart, blood-pressure or breathing conditions, factor the heat into your plans and talk to your doctor — and ease into outdoor activity, especially at first. See [staying active](/daily-life/staying-active-hobbies-and-sport/).

## The bottom line

Expect warmth year-round, a comfortable cool season, and short sharp rains rather than endless grey. Arrive in the cooler months to settle in comfortably — but be honest with yourself about the heat before you commit.
