Good hospitals are one of Pattaya's genuine strengths for retirees. Care is generally modern, fast, and far cheaper than equivalent private treatment back home — with English widely spoken.

## The main private hospitals

- **Bangkok Hospital Pattaya** — a large private hospital, part of a well-known national group, with a broad range of specialties and international-patient services. ⟨VERIFY current departments/accreditation⟩
- **Pattaya International Hospital** — long-established and centrally located, popular with expats for everyday and urgent care. ⟨VERIFY services⟩
- **Bangkok Hospital Jomtien** — convenient for those living in the quieter Jomtien area. ⟨VERIFY services⟩

There are also government hospitals (lower cost, busier, less English) and many clinics for minor issues.

## Quality vs cost — the honest view

For routine and many serious needs, the private hospitals here are excellent value: short waits, comfortable facilities, capable doctors. The trade-off isn't usually quality — it's that **you pay privately**, which is exactly why insurance (or a serious self-funding plan) matters so much. For the most complex or rare conditions, some patients still prefer Bangkok's largest centres.

## Practical tips

- Keep a **list of your medications** (generic names) and your history translated or summarised.
- Ask your insurer **which local hospitals they direct-bill**, so you're not paying large sums upfront and claiming back.
- For anything non-urgent, a **clinic** is often faster and cheaper than a hospital outpatient visit.

Pattaya's private hospitals are a real asset — modern, quick and good value. The catch is simply that care is paid-for, which makes your insurance or self-funding plan the thing to get right. See [health insurance by age](/healthcare/health-insurance-by-age/) next.
