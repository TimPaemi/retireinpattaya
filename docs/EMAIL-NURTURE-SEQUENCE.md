# Email Nurture Sequence — "The Honest Path"

A 7-email welcome + nurture flow in the brand voice: warm, calm, honest, never hype. Paste into your email provider (ConvertKit / MailerLite / Beehiiv) as an automation triggered when someone downloads a lead magnet or joins the list.

**Principles:** lead with honesty and usefulness, earn trust *before* any monetization, one clear action per email, always an easy unsubscribe. Health-insurance and visa/transfer links are where revenue lives — but they're framed as genuine help, never a pitch.

> Replace `[FIRST_NAME]`, links, and `[STARTER_KIT_LINK]` with your real values. Suggested timing in brackets — adjust to taste.

## Email 5 — Protecting your money (and dignity)  *(send: day 12)*

**Subject:** Why retirees are targeted — and how to stay safe
**Preview:** No judgement, just the red flags.

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

This one's protective, and we mean it kindly. Older newcomers are deliberately targeted by scams — romance, "guaranteed" investments, property cons. It's not about being naïve; these schemes are practised and persuasive.

The red flags are remarkably consistent:

- Money enters the picture, especially with urgency or a crisis story
- You're rushed, or discouraged from getting a second opinion
- Returns are "guaranteed"
- You're steered away from your own lawyer or adviser

Your defence is simple: **slow down, verify independently, and talk to someone you trust before committing serious money.**

## After the sequence — the ongoing list

Move subscribers to a light **broadcast list**: occasional updates when visa/tax/insurance rules change, new guides, and seasonal notes. Keep it rare and useful. Segment anyone who clicked the insurance or visa links for more relevant follow-ups.

## A note on deliverability & compliance

- Use a real double opt-in; it protects deliverability and trust.
- Every email needs a working unsubscribe and your physical sender address (legal requirement in most regions).
- Keep links consistent with your privacy policy. Never sell or share addresses — and say so.
